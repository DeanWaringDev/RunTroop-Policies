Safety & Security Policy

Last Updated: November 27, 2025

Our Mission: RunTroop exists to make group running safer through real-time location sharing and club coordination. Your safety is our top priority.

1. How RunTroop Enhances Safety

Real-Time Location Tracking

  • See everyone's position - Know where all group members are during runs
  • Updates every 5 seconds - Near real-time position tracking
  • Route paths shown - See the path each runner has taken
  • Last update time - Know when each position was recorded

Club-Based Organization

  • Controlled membership - Only club members can see your location
  • Designated leaders - Club owners can monitor the entire group
  • Planned routes - Upload and share routes in advance
  • Run history - Records of who participated in each run

Privacy Controls

  • Opt-in tracking - Location only shared when you join a run
  • Leave anytime - Stop sharing location instantly by leaving the run
  • Club-only visibility - Your location never visible outside your clubs
  • Automatic cleanup - Location data deleted after 30 days

2. Before the Run: Preparation

Club Owners Should:

  • Plan and upload routes in advance when possible
  • Ensure all participants have the app installed and working
  • Verify location permissions are enabled for all members
  • Set clear expectations about pace and difficulty
  • Establish emergency contact procedures
  • Know the route and potential hazards

Participants Should:

  • Ensure your device is fully charged
  • Enable location permissions in device settings
  • Test the app before the run starts
  • Bring a fully charged phone with cellular data
  • Know the planned route if available
  • Inform someone outside the club of your plans

General Running Safety

  • Wear visible/reflective clothing in low light
  • Carry identification
  • Bring water for longer runs
  • Know your physical limits
  • Check weather conditions
  • Be aware of your surroundings

3. During the Run: Active Tracking

Location Sharing Active: When you join a run, your location is visible to all other participants in real-time.

What Happens During Tracking

  • GPS updates sent every 5 seconds or when you move 20 meters
  • Your position shown on the map with your display name
  • Your route path drawn as you run
  • All participants can see each other's locations

Club Owner Responsibilities

  • Monitor all participants on the map
  • Check for anyone who stops moving unexpectedly
  • Notice if someone deviates significantly from the route
  • Be prepared to assist if someone has issues
  • End the run when everyone is safely finished

Participant Responsibilities

  • Keep your phone with you at all times
  • Maintain cellular data connection
  • Stay within a reasonable distance of the group
  • If you need to stop or leave, notify others
  • Use the "Leave Run" feature if departing early

4. Safety Scenarios and Responses

Scenario: Runner Stops Moving

What the map shows: Location marker hasn't updated in several minutes

Possible reasons:

  • Stopped for a rest (normal)
  • Device battery died
  • Lost cellular connection
  • Injury or medical issue

What to do:

  • Try calling or messaging the runner
  • Check their last known location
  • Send someone back to check if nearby
  • Call emergency services if unresponsive and potentially injured

Scenario: Runner Deviates from Route

What the map shows: Runner moving in unexpected direction

Possible reasons:

  • Took a different path (normal)
  • Running ahead or fell behind
  • Lost or disoriented
  • Emergency situation

What to do:

  • Contact the runner to check their status
  • Verify they know their location
  • Guide them back to the route if lost
  • Adjust plans if they're taking an alternate route

Scenario: Someone Needs Medical Help

Medical Emergency: Always call emergency services (999/911) immediately for serious injuries or health issues.

  • Call emergency services first
  • Share the exact GPS coordinates from the app
  • Send someone to the location if safe to do so
  • Provide first aid if trained
  • Keep the person comfortable until help arrives
  • Notify other group members

5. Technology Limitations

Important: RunTroop is a tool to enhance safety, not a guarantee. Technology can fail, and you should always use common sense.

GPS Accuracy

  • Typical accuracy: 5-15 meters in open areas
  • Reduced accuracy: Near tall buildings, in forests, or bad weather
  • Urban canyons: Skyscrapers can cause GPS drift
  • Tunnels: GPS won't work underground

Connectivity Requirements

  • Cellular data required: App needs internet to share locations
  • Coverage gaps: Rural areas may have spotty service
  • Network delays: Updates may be delayed during congestion
  • Roaming: International users may face restrictions

Battery Considerations

  • GPS drains battery: Expect 10-20% per hour during tracking
  • Cold weather: Batteries drain faster in cold
  • Older devices: May drain faster
  • Solution: Start with a fully charged device

6. Data Security Measures

How We Protect Your Location Data

  • Encrypted transmission: All data sent over HTTPS/TLS
  • Database encryption: Location data encrypted at rest
  • Access controls: Row Level Security enforces club-based permissions
  • No third-party sharing: Location never sold or shared outside RunTroop

Authentication Security

  • Password encryption: Passwords hashed with industry-standard algorithms
  • Leaked password protection: Blocks known compromised passwords
  • Secure sessions: Automatic timeout after inactivity
  • Device verification: Sessions tied to specific devices

What We Monitor

  • Unusual login patterns (potential account breach)
  • Excessive data requests (potential abuse)
  • Suspicious location patterns (potential tracking misuse)
  • Terms of service violations

7. Privacy Best Practices

Choose Clubs Carefully

  • Only join clubs with people you trust
  • Remember: all club members see each other's locations during runs
  • Leave clubs you're no longer active in
  • Report suspicious behavior to club owners

Control Your Visibility

  • Location only shared when you actively join a run
  • Leave the run immediately if you feel unsafe
  • You can leave clubs at any time
  • Delete your account if you want to stop using the service

Device Security

  • Use a strong, unique password
  • Enable device lock screen
  • Don't share your device with others while logged in
  • Log out on shared or public devices

8. Club Owner Responsibilities

With Great Power Comes Great Responsibility: Club owners have special duties to ensure member safety.

Member Safety

  • Verify new members are legitimate runners
  • Remove members who violate safety guidelines
  • Monitor all participants during active runs
  • Respond to safety concerns promptly
  • Establish clear safety protocols

Data Responsibility

  • Don't misuse access to member locations
  • Respect members' privacy outside of runs
  • Don't share location data outside the app
  • Handle member concerns professionally

Run Management

  • Plan safe routes appropriate for group skill level
  • End runs properly when complete
  • Don't start runs without adequate participation
  • Cancel runs if conditions are unsafe

9. Reporting Safety Concerns

If You Experience Harassment

  • Leave the club immediately
  • Block the user if possible
  • Report to safety@runtroop.app with details
  • Contact local authorities if you feel threatened

If You Witness Misuse

  • Document what you observed (screenshots if possible)
  • Report to the club owner first
  • If club owner is the issue, report to us
  • Email abuse@runtroop.app

If You Discover a Security Vulnerability

  • Do not exploit the vulnerability
  • Do not disclose it publicly
  • Report confidentially to security@runtroop.app
  • Provide details about how to reproduce it
  • We'll acknowledge within 24 hours

10. Safety Tips for Different Conditions

Running at Night

  • Wear reflective gear and carry a light
  • Stick to well-lit, populated routes
  • Run in larger groups for added safety
  • Be extra vigilant about traffic
  • Tell someone your planned route and time

Running in Heat

  • Start early or late to avoid peak temperatures
  • Bring extra water
  • Know signs of heat exhaustion
  • Slow your pace in extreme heat
  • Seek shade if you feel overheated

Running in Cold/Winter

  • Layer clothing appropriately
  • Keep your phone warm (battery drains faster)
  • Watch for ice on surfaces
  • Cover extremities (hands, ears, face)
  • Shorten runs in extreme cold

Running in Urban Areas

  • Be aware of traffic at all times
  • Use crosswalks and obey signals
  • Run facing traffic when on roads
  • Stay alert at intersections
  • Keep music/podcasts at safe volume

Running in Rural/Trail Areas

  • Tell someone your route beforehand
  • Bring offline maps (cellular may be limited)
  • Watch for wildlife
  • Be prepared for uneven terrain
  • Bring a whistle for emergencies

11. What RunTroop is NOT

Important Limitations: Understanding what RunTroop cannot do is crucial for your safety.

  • Not an emergency service: Cannot dispatch help or contact authorities
  • Not a medical device: Cannot detect injuries or health issues
  • Not a tracking device: Doesn't work when app is closed or location off
  • Not a guarantee: Cannot prevent accidents or ensure safety
  • Not a replacement for judgment: You must still make smart safety decisions
  • Not monitored 24/7: No one is watching your runs except your club members
